## Annual Meeting/Symposium

CytoDS supports the organization of an annual scientific meeting for the image-based profiling research community. The purpose of this meeting is to discuss recent advances in the field, foster communication within the community, and build connections with related domains by inviting experts from those fields to contribute to the symposium. The agenda is developed by the local organizers, while CytoDS provides support with fundraising, outreach, and general organizational matters. The meeting location must adhere to the society's equal rights policies.

### Selection of Organizers

Each year, CytoDS will issue a call for proposals to organize the annual meeting of the following year. Proposals must be submitted by May 31, 11:59 pm AOE, of year *n* for an event to be held in year *n+1*.

Proposals must be submitted by email to **cytodata.info@gmail.com** and should include:
- the name of the organization or organizations
- the location of the proposed event
- the name of the main coordinator or coordinators
- whether the host institution belongs to academia, industry, or another sector
- a brief description of the applicant's previous involvement with the CytoData community
- a brief statement describing the proposed event concept, including expected duration and content
- the proposed format of the event: in-person, hybrid, or online-only
- optionally, a 1-3 slide deck containing the key information the applicant would like to share

Applicants should note that the submitted information may be shared with community members for voting purposes.

The board will review all applications to ensure eligibility and may arbitrate if needed or extend the submission deadline.

If multiple eligible proposals are received, the host will be selected through a community vote using the Condorcet method. Voting will be open to all members registered on the society mailing list at the time the vote opens.

After the voting process, the selected host for year *n+1* will be announced during the annual meeting in year *n*.

Before receiving applications, or in the absence of suitable proposals, the board may announce this publicly and may choose to prioritize collaborations with other societies or interest groups.

CytoDS encourages geographic diversity in the selection of annual meeting locations, including hosting the meeting on different continents in consecutive years when possible. However, the final decision remains with the voters.
